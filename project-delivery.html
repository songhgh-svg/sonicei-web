// The Real Cost of Getting It Wrong

What Bad E&I Management Actually Costs

Most project overruns aren't caused by engineering complexity. They're caused by coordination failures, missed punch items, and subcontractors who don't own the outcome.

⏱
Schedule Overrun
E&I punch lists discovered late in commissioning are the #1 cause of final handover delays on power and offshore projects. A single missed instrument loop can hold up mechanical completion.
Avg delay: 3–6 weeks per unresolved punch cluster
💸
Rework Cost Cascade
A 1% rework rate on a $10M E&I contract is $100,000 in direct labour alone — before demurrage, specialist mobilisation costs, or client penalty clauses.
1% rework = $100K+ on a $10M contract
📋
Inadequate Documentation
Missing loop check records, unsigned test sheets, and untracked red-lines create audit exposure at handover and create liability for the client's O&M team for years after.
Documentation gaps = open liability post-handover
🔧
Unqualified Supervision
General sub-contractors without OEM spec knowledge (Siemens TI, GE EGS) introduce non-conformances that aren't visible until commissioning — or worse, until first fire.
Non-conformances found at FAT: x3–x5 cost to resolve

// Risk Transfer & Performance Guarantee

We Carry The Risk.

Engaging SONIC E&I is not just buying technical services — it's transferring E&I delivery risk to a contractor who backs their work with a contractual guarantee.

🛡
The Zero-Defect Guarantee
Any E&I defect attributable to our workmanship — termination quality, installation non-conformances, missed loop checks — is rectified at our cost. No change order. No negotiation.

This is not a best-effort commitment. It's a contractual clause written into every SONIC E&I project agreement, designed to protect you from the most common source of E&I cost overrun: post-handover defect discovery.
"Any defect in E&I workmanship attributable to SONIC E&I Solutions shall be remedied by SONIC E&I at no additional cost to the Client, within a mutually agreed timeframe."

— Standard clause, SONIC E&I Project Agreement
✓
Workmanship Defects → Our Cost
Crimp failures, loose terminations, incorrect wiring, missed grounds — if we installed it wrong, we fix it without billing a change order.
✓
Missed Punch Items → Our Accountability
Items that should have been caught in pre-comm but surface at handover inspection are resolved at our time and cost — not charged back as "additional scope."
📋
Documentation Gaps → We Close Them
Incomplete loop records, missing test sheets, or unsigned certificates — if they're within our scope, we rebuild the documentation before handover.
⚡
Schedule Risk → We Absorb Overtime
If our work causes a schedule delay and overtime is required to recover it, we resource the recovery. Your schedule is our schedule.
🔍
What the Guarantee Doesn't Cover
Client-supplied materials (cable, cable trays, instruments), design changes post-award, or defects in equipment supplied by third parties. We're transparent about the boundary.

// Common Questions From Technical Buyers

Answers You Actually Need

Your rates are higher than local subs. How do you justify that?
A 1% rework rate on a $10M E&I contract is $100K in direct labour — before demurrage or penalty clauses. Our premium over a standard sub is typically 15–25%. The break-even is one avoided rework event. We don't compete on day rate. We compete on total cost of delivery.
We already have an in-house team. Why do we need SONIC?
Most clients engage us for three reasons: their in-house team lacks Siemens/GE spec experience; they need a neutral QA/QC team for punch list closure; or they want AI-Punch™ deployed alongside their existing process. We're a precision add-on — not a replacement.
Can you really mobilise quickly if our schedule shifts?
Onshore Vũng Tàu / HCMC: 3–5 business days from PO. We hold a core team in reserve and don't over-book. If we can't commit to your window, we'll say so upfront — not take the work and miss.
How does the zero-defect guarantee work in practice?
It's a written clause in the contract, not a marketing promise. Any E&I defect attributable to our work is remedied at our cost. We define the boundary clearly at proposal stage — client-supplied materials and design changes are excluded. No ambiguity, no disputes at handover.
Do you work to international standards or only local?
We work to Siemens TI, GE EGS, IEC 60079, IEC 60364, and client-specified standards. Our founder has executed projects under Siemens Energy and GE Power quality management systems. The same technical standard. Without the overhead of a multinational contractor.
What if your team makes a mistake on a live system?
Mistakes on live systems don't happen because of carelessness — they happen because of inadequate pre-commission. Our structured approach — scope review and risk assessment before mobilisation, followed by full pre-commissioning checks (continuity, insulation, loop checks, instrument verification) before any energisation — is specifically designed to catch every risk before systems go live. Every live work activity is reviewed against the PTW and signed by the lead engineer.
